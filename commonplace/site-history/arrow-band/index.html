Pipestone-Jasper finished out their year with a performance that many would deem the best of the year in a strong effort at the Quad State marching band competition in Vermillion, South Dakota, this past weekend. Although the Arrows did not place first overall, they showed that they are truly a top tier band in beating all but one competitor in this huge competition.

The year can certainly be looked on as a success, especially considering all of the changes that have taken place. A veteran director left with the love of the community in his hand, and a new director came in and showed that he was truly meant to be back home in Pipestone. Congratulations to all the kids and Mr. Dan Stout for an outstanding year!

I would also like to apologize for the sporadic updates these past few weeks. The Brookings-Eden Prairie competition weekend was not something that would have been fun to report on. I did not feel the need to get any more political on this website than is already happening in the guestbook. :-) And of course, the college schedule keeps me busy.

The Pipestone-Jasper Marching Band has built a history and reputation as one of the best in the upper midwest. This comes from the excitement planted into each annual field show. The Arrows are not afraid to try something new and set the standard for high school bands in the area. The drive that pushes the Arrows to succeed is not necessarily for awards and honors, but more for the pride in the organization. Every Pipestone-Jasper Marching Band member is expected to give his/her best. The crowd's response is usually reward enough for the hard work that has been done during the summer and fall.
